# Inventory Reorder Rules

Inventory risk is highest when stock quantity is less than or equal to reorder level and the product has strong recent revenue. These products should be reviewed daily until replenishment arrives.

Reorder priority is based on three signals:

1. Revenue contribution over the last 90 days.
2. Stock gap, calculated as reorder level minus stock quantity.
3. Supplier lead time and reliability.

Critical items include high-demand Touring, 4WD Accessories, Lighting, Marine Audio, and Car Audio products where stock is below reorder level. Shower ensuites, roof rack platforms, LED light bars, roof top tents, marine head units, wake tower speakers, and component speaker sets should be escalated if they are low stock.

Reorder guidance: place purchase orders for critical items first, then shift available stock toward channels with stronger margin. Avoid heavy discounting on products with limited stock unless the item is being cleared.

Margin protection rule: if a supplier price increase occurred in the last quarter, confirm current sell price before reordering.
